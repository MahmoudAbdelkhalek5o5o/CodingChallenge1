"What is Love" is a dance-pop song by the artist Haddaway, released in 1993. The song explores the universal theme of love and relationships. Its catchy chorus, "What is love? Baby, don't hurt me, don't hurt me no more," poses the question of what love truly means and expresses a desire for love without pain or betrayal.

The song's upbeat tempo and infectious melody became iconic in the 1990s dance music scene, and it remains a popular choice for parties and playlists. Despite its catchy rhythm, the lyrics delve into deeper emotional territory, reflecting on the complexities and uncertainties of romantic love.

Overall, "What is Love" captures the timeless quest for understanding love's meaning and the yearning for genuine connection without heartache.
